B. Cocktail Table

 

Cocktail tables are 2-person machines (though a 4-person is possible) that are reminiscent of the old Ms. Pacman and other arcade machines from the 80's where the user will sit down to play them.  Below are photos of 2 different, 2-person cocktail table designs.  This is the one EHA machine that does not play all of the games uprights, Pedestals, Table Tops, and GameBoxes can play.  This is because light gun games, wii games and some others do not work well on the horizontal screen of Cocktail Tables.   They do play games with other peripherals such as X-box controllers.  

Below is an overview of Cocktail Tables.  In addition, their positives and negatives (compared against other EHA machines) are covered in this section.

 

Cocktail Tables:

  • They are 30” W x 34” D x 31” H and sit on the floor (see photos below).  While they are shorter than the uprights, they are still quite heavy at 225 pounds.
  • They come in two different, 2-person styles cabinets.
  • The TV is 32" 1080p.
  • The machine comes with a standard sound system (including subwoofer) and accepts headphones.  When the headphones are being used the machine is very quiet. The joysticks and buttons make very little noise.
  • Like any PC, it's computer hardware can be upgraded and replaced

Cocktail Tables Positives (versus other EHA machines): 

  • They are a sit-down style machine.
  • Cocktail tables are an all-in-one system.
  • Cocktail tables have some room for graphics on the cabinet (but not as much as an upright).
  • They plays some, but not all, of the same games as uprights and pedestals.
  • They can accommodate a neon kick plate.

 

Cocktail Tables Negatives (versus other EHA machines): 

  • They are heavy at 225 pounds.
  • Cocktail Tables have small cabinets so when you need to work in the cabinet on the computer space is tight.
  • They have smaller TV screens (32") than the larger uprights.
  • The tight space in the cabinet as the potential to create heat concerns for the computer motherboard and graphic board (this can be solved for by additional fans or a cooling system if that occurs).
  • Cocktail tables cannot play all of the games other machines can play due to the horizontal screen.
  • Flight sticks, steering wheel/pedals, light guns, Wii controllers, guitars and dance pads do not work at all or do not work well with cocktail tables.
  • There is no option for a  passive or active marquee.

C. Computer Upgrades 

 

EHA allows customers to upgrade their base computer system as an option. Generally speaking, this is a good idea and allows users to â€œfuture proof" their computer.  That is, as new emulators and games come out with new hardware demands your computer will be able to handle the new demands into the future.   Below is a summary of the computer upgrades:

 

 - GTX 1660 GPU - This is an NVIDIA GTX 1660 super graphics processing unit.  It is a graphics card designed  for gaming.  It has dual fans on the board to keep it cool.   Some of the newer games on Omega will benefit from the enhanced power of the NVIDIA GTX 1660 graphics card.  As new games continue to come out with more and more complex graphics, this card will help the EHA system stay current.

 

- Core i7 (CPU) - Standard EHA machine come with an Intel 10th Gen i5 10400F processor.   The Intel Core i7 CPU provides additional computer horsepower for complex gaming. Again, the newer games on Omega will benefit from the increased computing power from the Core i7.  Your system will run faster and not lag, at least as compared to the standard i5 processor.

 

- 1 TB Boot Drive - The standard EHA boot drive is 500 Gb.  The 1 TB boot drives is required for Omega. An option is to purchase a 2Tb boot drive.   This is just a few dollars more and is recommended.

 

- 8Tb Omega Expansion Drive -  This adds 100+ new customer requested PC games, more PS3 games and movies/documentaries.

 

- Omega Drive (16 TB) - The Omega drive is the newest upgraded hard drive that EHA offers.  It has many enhancement over past drives and is highly recommended. In addition, when you upgrade to Omega included in the price is the addition of 8Gb of DDR4 RAM.  This will take your machine to 16 Gb of DDR4 RAM.
